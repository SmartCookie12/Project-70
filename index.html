Campus Life

School Cafeteria

Opened in 1998

From 1998 to 2023, the school cafeteria has served millions of students, tolerated their messes and accidents, and gave them a place to talk and laugh. First opened on the school's first mid year festival, the beautiful cafeteria had shone and sparkle, and served healthy meals for hungry students after their lessons. But after surviving over 20 years of students in 2018, the place needed cleaning up. But with the help of the student council, and the clean up crew, the school was able to make the cafeteria prettier than it's ever been.



School Auditorium

Opened in 1999

In the wonderful school auditorium, we host competitions, debates, and assemblies. We also perform class plays there in winter and spring, and we also watch fun movies there on school movie night. A few years ago, a tragedy happened in our auditorium. There was a fire, a small one, but big enough to destroy half of the place. But this year, we have a newly reinnovated auditorium, that is more wonderful than ever!



School Library

Opened in 2003

Opened on the school's 5th anniversary, the library has always been helpful for both students and teacher. A calm place for kids to relax and learn, and a peaceful place for teachers to plan their lesson plans. Each grade has a special day to visit the library, pick books, and listen to a story read by the librarian.

Achievements

Inter School Debate About Pollution
Andrew Brown won first place in the inter school debate competition

Last few minutes of the debate competition. There is tight competition between Andrew Brown and Jessica Drew. But at the end, talented Andrew Brown took the gold. The topic was about pollution. Andrew was talking about how we should use metal bottles instead of plastic, while Jessica Drew was siding with plastic bottles.

Inter School Football Competition
Stratford School team takes over Merryhill in an epic Football Competition

A very close call. In the last few minutes of the game, it didn't seem possible that Stratford would win. The Stratford Party Committee almost decided to drop the big celebration they had planned to celebrate the victory. But one last move from our star quarterback brought us to victory!

Inter School Science Fair
Who Will Represent Our School at the STR SciClub?

This year we had some exceptional projects from the middle schoolers. Top three was a bacteria growth project by Elena, an apple oxidation project by Riley, and a levitating train made out of magnets by Ryan. But at last, the most deserved was chosen by the judges to win a scholarship in a special science club for smart kids. Congratulations, Riley! She will now represent our school in STR SciClub. Yay!
